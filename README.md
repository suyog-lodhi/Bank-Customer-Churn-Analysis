# 🏦 Bank Customer Churn & Financial Risk Analysis | Power BI Project

## 📌 Executive Summary

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges in the banking industry as it directly impacts revenue, profitability, and long-term customer relationships. This project analyzes 10,000 bank customers to identify churn drivers, measure financial impact, and uncover opportunities to improve retention strategies.

The analysis reveals that churn is primarily driven by **customer inactivity and weak product relationships**, with a disproportionate financial impact caused by the loss of high-value customers. The bank has already lost **$186M (24%) of its managed balance** due to churn, with Germany showing the highest financial risk.

The project demonstrates how customer behaviour analytics can be used to identify churn risks early and support data-driven retention strategies.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Banks invest heavily in acquiring customers, but retention remains a major challenge. Losing high-value customers not only reduces deposits but also affects long-term profitability.

Key business questions addressed:

- What factors drive customer churn?
- Which customers are most at risk?
- What financial impact does churn create?
- Which customer segments require retention focus?
- How can the bank reduce churn risk?

## 🔍 Key Findings

## Customer Behaviour Insights

**1**
Inactive customers churn at nearly **twice the rate** of active customers, confirming engagement as the strongest churn predictor.

**2**
Almost half of customers are inactive, creating a large future churn risk pool.

**3**
Most churned customers (64%) were inactive before leaving, showing churn is often preceded by disengagement.

**4**
High balance customers appear among inactive segments, indicating early warning signs of financial loss.

---

## Financial Risk Insights

**5**
Customer churn has resulted in **$186M loss**, representing **24% of total managed balance**.

**6**
Germany shows the highest financial churn exposure with **33% balance loss**, making it the highest risk geography.

**7**
High value customers represent a major share of churn, showing the bank is losing financially important relationships.

**8**
France (441), Germany (397), and Spain (235) lost large numbers of high-value customers.

**9**
Churned customers hold higher balances than retained customers, indicating poor retention of valuable clients.

---

## Product Insights

**10**
Customers with only one product account for the highest financial losses.

**11**
Multi-product customers show lower churn, confirming relationship depth improves retention.

**12**
Average products per customer is only two, indicating cross-sell opportunities.

**13**
Credit card ownership alone does not significantly reduce churn.

---

## Customer Segment Insights

**14**
Customers aged 36-55 create the highest financial losses.

**15**
Tenure does not strongly protect against churn.

**16**
Salary and credit score show weak correlation with churn compared to behavioural factors.

## 💡 Business Recommendations

### 1 Customer Engagement Strategy
Focus on inactive customers through:
- Targeted campaigns
- Usage incentives
- Digital engagement programs

### 2 Cross Sell Programs
Encourage customers to hold multiple products through:
- Product bundles
- Relationship discounts
- Loyalty incentives

### 3 High Value Customer Protection
Implement:
- Priority banking services
- Dedicated support
- Personalized offers

### 4 Geographic Retention Strategy
Investigate Germany churn causes and deploy region-specific retention strategies.

### 5 Early Churn Detection
Use inactivity and product ownership as early churn indicators.

### 6 Product Expansion Strategy
Target single-product customers for relationship deepening.
